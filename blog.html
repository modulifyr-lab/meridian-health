Understanding Your Annual Health Check: What to Ask For and Why

Most people book an annual health check and leave the specifics entirely to their doctor — which is fine, but it means missing an opportunity to get more out of thirty minutes than a standard checklist covers.

A good annual check should be tailored to your age, family history, and risk factors, not run as a generic template. That means asking direct questions: which screenings actually apply to you this year, what your baseline numbers mean over time rather than in isolation, and what's worth monitoring given your specific history.

Below, we walk through the questions worth raising at your next visit, and why they matter more than the standard panel alone.
